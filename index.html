I am a Postdoctoral Associate at the Center for Brains Minds and Machines at MIT. I got my PhD in Electrical and Computer Engineering at Johns Hopkins University, where I was a member of the Digital Signal Processing Laboratory, advised by Prof. Trac D. Tran. Before coming to JHU, I graduated with a B.Tech in Electrical Engineering from IIT Madras

My research interests are at the intersection of the theory of machine learning, signal processing, and optimization. More specifically, I am interested in the understanding deep neural networks from a theoretical perspective and verifying theoretical conclusions through rigorous experimentation. I have also worked on problems in sparse and low rank signal processing, with applications to computer vision, biomedical signal processing, and online social networks.
